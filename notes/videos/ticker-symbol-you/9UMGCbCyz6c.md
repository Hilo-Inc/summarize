### Quantum Computing 101

- **Classical bit** = coin showing heads or tails (0 or 1).
- **Qubit** = weighted coin in superposition; carries probabilities of 0 and 1 until measured.
- **Entanglement**: linked qubits share patterns instead of flipping independently. *n* entangled qubits → **2ⁿ** possible states. **300 entangled qubits = more states than atoms in the observable universe.**
- **Decoherence**: heat, vibration, or a stray photon collapses qubits back to classical bits. Keeping them stable long enough for useful computation is the whole game.

Best-suited problem classes: **optimization, simulation, cryptography, certain ML workloads**.

### Rigetti Computing (RGTI) — "The Nvidia-stack play"

- **Tech**: Superconducting qubits (like Google, IBM) — tiny circuits cooled near absolute zero, behaving as artificial atoms. ~4,000× faster than trapped ions, but errors push real-world advantage down to ~10×; much harder to scale.
- **Edge**: Only **pure-play** way to invest in superconducting quantum (Google/IBM bury it inside larger businesses). Plugged into **NVQ Link** and **CUDA-Q**; first 100+ qubit processor on AWS.
